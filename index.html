What Real Yield Means

Real yield measures the inflation-adjusted return on a bond. It helps investors understand whether a nominal bond return is preserving purchasing power after inflation.

Approximate vs Exact

The approximate method subtracts inflation from nominal yield. The exact method applies a more precise inflation adjustment using the Fisher-style relationship between nominal and real returns.

Methodology

The approximate formula is: real yield ≈ nominal yield − inflation rate.

The exact formula is: real yield = ((1 + nominal yield) / (1 + inflation rate)) − 1.
